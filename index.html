My Thinking

Principles I design
AI experiences by

01 🧭
Behavior before interface

The hardest design decisions in AI are behavioral, not visual. What should the AI say in this moment? How certain should it sound? When should it stop and let the user decide?

02 🌡️
Tone is a system decision

In AI products, tone cannot be left to individual writers. It needs to be defined early, encoded in guidelines, and tested against real outputs before anything ships.

03 🔒
Design the failure modes first

A well-designed AI feature spends as much time on what goes wrong as what goes right. Guardrails, fallbacks, and recovery paths deserve the same design rigor as the core flow.

04 📏
Shared patterns over isolated flows

The most valuable design work is the kind that many teams can use. A well-documented pattern or component guideline does more for a product organization than a dozen one-off designs.

05 🔍
Trust is earned through transparency

Users don't trust AI because it's fast. They trust it when it shows its reasoning, cites where it got its information, and is honest about what it doesn't know.

06 👤
Start with the user, work backward

The right question is never "what can the model do?" It's "what does the user actually need?" Good AI design starts with that and works backward through technical constraints to find what's worth building.

How I Work

Research first,
then design to ship

01 🔬
Research & Discovery

Partnering with UX Research to understand user pain points, identify opportunity areas, and audit existing behavior before putting anything on the canvas. I want to know what's actually broken before I start designing.

02 🗺️
User Journeys & Flows

Building user journeys, interaction flows, and wireframes that map out the full experience. I use these to surface assumptions early, align the team, and give engineering something concrete to react to.

03 ⚙️
Prototype & Present

High-fidelity prototypes and stakeholder presentations that make the design real. I test with users, refine based on what I learn, and make sure the rationale is documented and defensible.

04 📡
Partner with Engineering

Working closely with engineers to implement designs to spec, find creative solutions to technical constraints, and contribute final patterns back to shared design systems for other teams to use.
